# Appeals Packet Playbook

Work Order: `WO-COUNTY-008`

## Purpose

Define a complete, neutral preparation packet for administrative or judicial
valuation review. It does not decide a real appeal or replace counsel.

## Packet Sections

1. authority, forum, deadlines, and scope of review;
2. case identity and protected-data classification;
3. issue statement and relief requested;
4. procedural timeline and notices;
5. property and valuation-date facts;
6. appellant claims and submitted evidence;
7. assessor record and valuation method;
8. comparable, cost, and income support as applicable;
9. responses to each material claim;
10. contradictions, missing evidence, and limitations;
11. exhibit index with source and confidentiality;
12. proposed factual stipulations;
13. hearing or conference preparation notes;
14. decision/order and implementation instructions;
15. later appeal, correction, refund, or roll-change evidence.

## Preparation Rules

- Use the controlling assessment year and valuation date.
- Preserve the appellant’s wording accurately.
- Separate procedural issues from valuation issues.
- Link every factual statement to an exhibit or approved system source.
- Explain adjustments and model outputs in plain language.
- Include evidence that cuts against the assessor’s position.
- Identify the limits of inspection, access, and available market evidence.
- Use respectful, bias-free language.
- Keep draft strategy, privileged communications, and protected records in
  their authorized locations—not in a public packet.
- Require a final completeness and redaction review.

## Completion Checklist

- jurisdiction and deadline verified;
- record complete;
- each claim answered;
- calculations reproduced;
- exhibits legible and indexed;
- protected information handled;
- reviewer sign-off recorded;
- delivery or filing evidence retained;
- decision implementation and rollback/correction path assigned.

## Boundary

This page does not establish deadlines, waive defenses, make admissions,
determine value, provide legal advice, file a petition, transmit evidence, or
change a tax roll. Specific matters require current law, adopted procedure,
authorized officials, and counsel where appropriate.
