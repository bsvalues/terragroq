# Board of Equalization Evidence Reference

Work Order: `WO-COUNTY-004`

## Purpose

Define a neutral and reproducible evidence structure for valuation appeals
without storing or deciding a real case.

Current starting authorities include
[RCW 84.40.038](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=84.40.038) and
[chapter 458-14 WAC](https://app.leg.wa.gov/WAC/default.aspx?cite=458-14).
Operational users must verify current filing, notice, hearing, confidentiality,
and record requirements.

## Packet Structure

1. case-control sheet and timeline;
2. petition, requested value, and asserted grounds;
3. valuation notice and relevant assessment-year facts;
4. subject description with source provenance;
5. assessor valuation method and calculation lineage;
6. taxpayer evidence inventory;
7. assessor evidence inventory;
8. comparable-selection criteria and adjustment support;
9. cost or income support when relied upon;
10. inspection, access, and condition evidence;
11. issues and stipulations;
12. neutral response to each material claim;
13. exhibits and exhibit index;
14. hearing notes and board order;
15. later correction, appeal, or implementation evidence.

## Evidence Rules

- Separate facts, assumptions, analysis, and conclusions.
- Address the taxpayer’s actual contention rather than substituting a generic
  defense.
- Use the valuation date and assessment year that control the case.
- Explain why each comparable or method is relevant.
- Preserve both favorable and unfavorable evidence.
- Identify unavailable information and its effect on confidence.
- Avoid advocacy language, protected-class references, and unsupported
  credibility judgments.
- Redact or restrict protected information before a packet is shared outside
  its authorized case context.

## Quality Gate

A reviewer should be able to answer:

- What value is contested and for which year?
- What did each party submit?
- What authority and valuation methods apply?
- Can every material number be traced?
- Were contradictions and limitations disclosed?
- Does the response address each claim?
- Is the packet complete enough to reproduce the analysis?

## Boundary

This page does not establish filing timeliness, decide admissibility, make a
legal conclusion, determine value, or disclose a case record. Those decisions
remain with the authorized officials and controlling law.
