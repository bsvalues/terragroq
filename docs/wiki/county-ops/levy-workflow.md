# Levy Workflow Reference

Work Order: `WO-COUNTY-003`

## Purpose

Provide a reviewable levy-workflow model without calculating or certifying a
real levy.

Washington levy work should begin with current authority, including
[chapter 84.52 RCW](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=84.52) and
current [Department of Revenue property-tax guidance](https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/property-tax).

## Governed Workflow

1. **Open the levy year.** Record tax year, assessment year, run date, operator,
   software version, and current authority set.
2. **Inventory districts.** Reconcile district identity, type, boundaries,
   annexations, dissolutions, and intercounty status.
3. **Collect lawful inputs.** Preserve resolutions, ordinances, budgets,
   certifications, ballot measures, statutory factors, and corrections.
4. **Validate taxable value.** Separate district value, new construction,
   state-assessed utility value, refunds, omitted property, destroyed property,
   and other authorized components.
5. **Calculate in review mode.** Record every input, formula, limit, proration,
   protection, and intermediate result.
6. **Run exception review.** Flag missing certifications, inconsistent district
   identity, unexpected rate movement, negative components, threshold
   crossings, and unresolved legal questions.
7. **Independent review.** A second reviewer reproduces material results from
   source evidence.
8. **Prepare certification evidence.** Preserve the approved result, source
   documents, reviewer sign-off, exceptions, and correction path.
9. **Reconcile after extension.** Confirm certified values and rates correspond
   to the tax-roll outcome without overwriting the original evidence.

## Minimum Evidence Packet

- district identity and authority;
- levy request and receipt date;
- source resolutions and ballot authority;
- assessed-value components and provenance;
- calculation worksheet or reproducible model version;
- statutory-limit and proration analysis;
- exception report;
- independent review;
- final certification and correction history.

## Controls

- Never infer a levy request from last year.
- Never silently substitute missing inputs.
- Never hide a proration or correction inside a rounded final rate.
- Separate legal authority from arithmetic.
- Preserve original submissions and every later correction.
- Treat intercounty allocation and boundary changes as explicit review lanes.
- Do not publish a taxpayer-specific estimate from this reference model.

## Boundary

This page does not calculate, approve, certify, extend, or transmit a levy.
Any live levy action requires the county’s approved systems, current DOR
guidance, adopted policy, and authorized reviewers.
