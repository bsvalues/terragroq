## /goal

Purpose: make Washington county-assessor operating knowledge visible,
reviewable, and teachable inside WilliamOS without connecting to or mutating a
county system.

Desired state:

- one County Ops knowledge map;
- bounded PACS read-only doctrine;
- levy, BOE, permit, public-records, ratio-study, and appeals references;
- consistent evidence, redaction, provenance, and authority boundaries;
- Academy/Wiki discoverability;
- no real parcel, owner, taxpayer, appeal, permit, exemption, or credential
  data.

Standing Codex authority:

- create and maintain static documentation, read-only registry records, tests,
  evidence, and navigation;
- use current public Washington sources;
- manage the normal branch, PR, review, eligible merge, post-merge evidence,
  and continuation chain.

Blocked:

- PACS connectivity, query execution, exports, writes, or credentials;
- county network, VPN, servers, databases, file shares, email, or production;
- parcel, owner, taxpayer, exemption, permit, BOE, or assessment case data;
- legal conclusions for a specific case;
- TerraFusion integration or mutation;
- auth, DB/schema/data, env/package/Vercel, deploy, release, or tag changes;
- command runner, background worker, scheduler, Hermes/MCP/skill activation,
  memory runtime, dynamic ingestion, RAG, embeddings, vector storage, or
  autonomy.

## /loop

Mode: sequential with a coherent static-documentation batch.

Continue until: goal complete or a typed authority wall.

Selection rule: complete the first incomplete Work Order in declared order.

Validation rule:

- inspect changed-file scope and public-source provenance;
- verify all examples are generic and contain no real records;
- verify no operational connection instructions or credentials are present;
- inspect links, identifiers, Markdown whitespace, and secret patterns;
- require remote checks and zero unresolved substantive review threads;
- verify merged files on `origin/main`.

Stop walls:

- any need for real county information;
- any operational PACS or county-system access;
- any specific legal conclusion, disclosure decision, or case outcome;
- any runtime, persistence, external-service, production, or higher-risk
  implementation.

## Source Doctrine

Operational staff must verify current law, Department of Revenue guidance,
county policy, and counsel direction before using a reference in a live matter.
The official source links in this pack are starting points, not frozen copies
of law and not legal advice.

Primary public sources:

- [Washington Title 84 RCW](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=84)
- [Washington Department of Revenue property tax](https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/property-tax)
- [Washington Title 458 WAC](https://app.leg.wa.gov/WAC/default.aspx?cite=458)
- [Washington Public Records Act, chapter 42.56 RCW](https://app.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=42.56)
